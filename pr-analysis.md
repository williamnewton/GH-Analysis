## Learnings & Observations

### 1. You're a polish machine
The single largest bucket is UI Polish & Styling. This suggests you play a critical role in taking features from "functional" to "shippable." Many of these PRs are small, targeted fixes — padding, z-index, dark mode, spacing — that collectively make a huge difference in product quality.

### 2. Velocity correlates with product launches
The ramp from 14 PRs in December to 33 in February tracks what looks like a Global Agent / AI Controls launch push. February was dominated by splash pages, rebranding (Ask AI → Global Agent), availability states, and polish — classic pre-launch work.

### 3. Feature → Polish → Iterate cycle
There's a clear pattern: new features land first (e.g., Agents dashboard entrypoint in late Jan), followed by waves of polish PRs (styling, spacing, dark mode). This is a healthy development pattern — ship the feature, then refine.

### 4. You work across the full stack of UI concerns
Your PRs span `[lightning]` (main app), `[ai-chat-core]` (chat components), `[onenav]` (navigation), `[agents]` (new product area), `[langley]` (backend/AI), `[i18n]`, and `[stargate]`. This breadth suggests you're a key contributor who can context-switch across the UI surface.

### 5. Small PRs = fast shipping
Almost every PR is single-purpose: one fix, one tweak, one feature addition. This keeps reviews fast and reduces blast radius. The tradeoff is a higher PR count, but that's worth it for a product under active development.

### 6. The Opportunities feature is the next big bet
Starting in early March, there's a distinct cluster of `[agents]` PRs focused on Opportunities — detail pages, cards with metrics, status groups, an investigator subagent. This is clearly a new product area ramping up, moving from initial build to refinement.

### 7. Naming/branding work is real work
Several PRs are dedicated to renaming "Ask AI" → "Chat" → "Global Agent" across i18n strings, UI, and navigation. Branding pivots generate real engineering work that's easy to underestimate.
